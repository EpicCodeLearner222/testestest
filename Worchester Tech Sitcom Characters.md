# Episodes List
<details>
  <summary>Pilot Episode - S1E1</summary>
 
  ## **Pilot Episode Overview — *Worchester Tech: “The New Year”***

The pilot episode introduces the chaotic, mockumentary-style world of **Worchester Technical High School**, where a new teacher’s optimism collides with a principal who treats the school like her personal kingdom and a staff that has already given up on expecting competence.

---

### **🎬 Core Setup**
The episode centers on **Justine**, a cheerful and prepared teacher transferred from an elementary school. She arrives ready to make a fresh start—only to discover that the school is run by **Principal Avah**, a dramatic, self-absorbed leader who refuses handshakes, avoids responsibility, and fires her secretary at least once a day.

As Justine decorates her classroom and meets her coworkers, she quickly realizes that the school’s biggest problem isn’t the students—it’s the administration.

---

### **🏫 Key Characters Introduced**
- **Justine** — optimistic, organized, and determined to make a good impression.  
- **Principal Avah** — flamboyant, egotistical, allergic to work, and constantly performing for the camera.  
- **Barb** — veteran teacher who has seen everything and has no patience left for Avah’s theatrics.  
- **Dian** — the perpetually “fired” secretary who keeps the school running despite Avah.  
- **Daniel** — the janitor who quietly observes the madness.  
- **Zach** — a new senior transferring from another school.

---

### **📌 What Happens (Without Spoilers)**
The pilot follows Justine’s first day as she:
- Sets up her classroom and meets the staff.  
- Witnesses Avah’s dramatic “ghost sighting” when a white family arrives for a tour.  
- Attends a chaotic staff meeting where Avah announces budget cuts, layoffs, and—most importantly—her own raise.  
- Survives her first class, where a student immediately curses her out.  
- Joins the breakroom crew, where teachers bond over Avah’s daily meltdowns.  

Meanwhile, Avah avoids all responsibility, hides in her office, and becomes overly dramatic when the district calls asking about layoffs.

---

### **🎭 Tone & Style**
The pilot blends:
- Dry humor  
- Exaggerated workplace dysfunction  
- Deadpan interviews  
- Chaotic school energy  

It sets up a world where the staff holds the school together *despite* its leadership, and where every day feels like a new episode of Avah’s personal reality show.

---

### **✨ What the Pilot Establishes**
- The dysfunctional dynamic between Avah and the staff  
- Justine’s struggle to adapt to high school life  
- The mockumentary format and character interviews  
- Ongoing tension around layoffs, responsibility, and school culture

  ## **Episode 2 Overview — *Worchester Tech: “Classroom Shuffle”***  

Episode 2 picks up the morning after the chaotic pilot, following Justine as she tries—once again—to start her day on a positive note. Her optimism lasts about thirty seconds before Avah storms into the building, treating the camera crew like her personal paparazzi and pushing Justine out of frame. From there, the episode becomes a slow‑burn disaster centered around one simple problem: Avah has decided Justine must switch classrooms… for absolutely no logical reason.

---

### **🎬 Core Setup**
Justine begins the day in the breakroom, sharing a wholesome moment with Barb—her teaching role model—before Avah barges in and derails the vibe. The mockumentary cameras capture every awkward beat as Avah demands attention, flicks her hair, and treats the school like a runway.

---

### **🏫 Main Storyline**
The episode’s central conflict begins when Avah interrupts Justine’s lesson over the intercom, summoning her to the office with unnecessary urgency. Once Justine arrives, Avah casually announces that she’s being moved to a different classroom, despite Justine having just finished setting up her room last week.

The rest of the episode follows Justine’s increasingly frustrating attempts to:
- Get a straight answer from Avah  
- Figure out where her new classroom even *is*  
- Manage her students while Avah publicly embarrasses her over the PA system  
- Keep her composure as the entire school hears every humiliating moment  

The intercom becomes a running gag—Avah uses it like a personal walkie‑talkie, responding to things Justine mutters under her breath as if she has supernatural hearing.

---

### **😂 Tone & Style**
Episode 2 leans into:
- Deadpan reactions  
- Over-the-top intercom announcements  
- Awkward silences  
- Justine’s growing “I’m so done with this” energy  

It expands the mockumentary feel by showing how even a simple task—moving classrooms—turns into a full‑scale disaster under Avah’s leadership.

---

### **✨ What This Episode Establishes**
- The deepening mentor‑mentee bond between Barb and Justine  
- Avah’s escalating misuse of power and technology  
- The school’s culture of chaos, where even routine tasks become catastrophes  
- Justine’s slow realization that she may be trapped in the most dysfunctional school in the district
